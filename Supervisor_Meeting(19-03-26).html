Sign Language Recognition System

Student: Shivam

Supervisor: Dr. Yanling Hao

Project: Deep Learning-Based Sign Language Recognition System

Weekly Summary (Bullet Points)

  • Conducted an extensive literature review to understand the problem domain of sign language recognition and its social impact (LeCun, Bengio & Hinton, 2015; Goodfellow, Bengio & Courville, 2016).
  • Studied deep learning fundamentals, including Artificial Neural Networks, forward/backpropagation, and activation functions such as ReLU and Softmax (LeCun et al., 2015).
  • Developed understanding of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and their layered structure for hierarchical feature extraction (Krizhevsky, Sutskever & Hinton, 2012; Simonyan & Zisserman, 2015).
  • Investigated computer vision and image processing techniques: resizing, normalization, noise reduction, background simplification (Szeliski, 2010).
  • Explored hand detection frameworks like MediaPipe and OpenCV for reliable hand landmark detection in real-time (Zhang et al., 2020).
  • Analyzed real-time system design: low latency, continuous input, modular architecture, performance optimization (Howard et al., 2017; Pigou et al., 2015).
  • Planned model training methodology: dataset preparation, augmentation, CNN training, categorical cross-entropy loss, Adam optimizer, dropout layers, early stopping (Goodfellow et al., 2016; Krizhevsky et al., 2012).
  • Evaluated performance metrics: accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, confusion matrices (Sokol & Flach, 2020).
  • Explored transfer learning with pre-trained models (VGG16, ResNet50) to improve performance and reduce training time (Simonyan & Zisserman, 2015).
  • Conducted reflection and planning for future steps: transition to dynamic gestures, real-time system integration, user testing.
  • Considered additional challenges: variations in lighting, hand sizes, complex backgrounds; planned mitigation strategies like normalization, preprocessing, efficient CNN architectures.
  • Documented a clear roadmap for project integration: video capture → hand detection → preprocessing → CNN prediction → text/speech output.
  • Prepared for supervisor meeting to discuss progress, receive feedback, and refine implementation strategies.
